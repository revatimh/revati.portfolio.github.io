Connecting Flights

Understanding Passenger Rights and Flight Disruption Experiences

A UX research project uncovering the communication gap between airlines and passengers during flight disruptions.

Overview

This research focuses on the communication gap between airlines and passengers, especially during flight disruptions. It examines the reasons behind the unawareness of passengers about their rights and compensation knowledge, which often leads to frustration and dissatisfaction.

By analyzing their expectations and experiences, the study aims to improve airline communication, reduce confusion, and enhance overall customer trust. Additionally, it proposes solutions to minimize issues, ensuring a smoother and more informed travel experience.

Problem Statement

What are the factors contributing to the communication gap between passengers’ experiences and their expectations during a flight disruption?

Objectives

  • Identify information barriers for passengers and explore ways to establish clarity.
  • Analyze how past experiences shape passenger expectations.
  • Examine airline policies and passenger rights to improve cooperation.
  • Assess whether passengers’ experiences differ from expectations during disruptions.

Existing Solutions and Limitations

  • Compensation System: Monetary compensation exists, but many passengers are unaware of it.
  • Quality of Service: Airline staff receive training but communication inconsistencies remain.
  • Lack of Awareness: Passengers often only discover their rights during disruptions.
  • Lack of Communication: Airlines disclose limited or unclear details.

Demographic Details

The study focused on passengers who:

  • Were between 18–30 years old
  • Flew internationally in the last two years
  • Traveled in economy class
  • Booked tickets in one package
  • Had at least one connecting flight
  • Often traveled alone

Research Methodology

The project used a mixed-methods approach combining qualitative and quantitative research:

  • Secondary Research: Literature review and existing policy documents
  • Survey: 107 responses
  • Passenger Interviews: 12 participants
  • SME Interviews: 4 subject matter experts
  • Validation Interviews: 3 participants

Findings and Key Insights

1. Communication Gaps

Passengers lose trust due to insufficient information during disruptions.

“Knowing nothing is more concerning than knowing the truth.”

Opportunity: Rebuild trust through transparent communication.

2. Lack of Awareness of Rights

Many passengers were unaware of APPR (Air Passenger Protection Rights).

“I would have expected compensation but I assume this was not contemplated in the law.”

Opportunity: Create awareness about passenger rights.

3. Complex and Lengthy Documentation

Only 21.9% of respondents read the APPR; most found it difficult.

“It’s like reading a textbook. It would be better if important points were highlighted.”

Opportunity: Simplify the APPR and highlight key points.

4. Confusion Over Eligibility

56.1% of passengers were entitled to compensation, but only 29.2% pursued it.

“So it’s like what needs to happen for me to have the right to get compensation?”

Opportunity: Develop tools to clarify eligibility and processes.

Experience Principles

  • Transparency: Honest updates about disruptions.
  • Awareness: Easy access to rights information.
  • Clarity: Simple, digestible regulations.
  • Eligibility: Clear guidance on compensation.

How Might We?

  • Improve airline communication despite disclosure limits?
  • Make APPR more understandable?
  • Simplify eligibility information and make it accessible?
  • Create awareness about passenger protection rights?

Proposed Solution: Project Inform-Me-Right

Goals:

  1. Create awareness about APPR.
  2. Provide a website with:
    • Compensation eligibility checker
    • Simplified APPR guide

Awareness Campaigns

  • Posters and digital ads at airports
  • Brochures in seat pockets
  • In-flight entertainment system ads

Website Features

  • Eligibility Checker: Passengers input disruption details → get instant results.
  • Simplified APPR: Clear navigation, plain language, short sections, highlighted key points.

Storyboard (Solution in Action)

  1. Passenger experiences a delay and feels stressed.
  2. Notices promotional material for Inform-Me-Right.
  3. Scans QR code → accesses website.
  4. Reads simplified rights and checks eligibility.
  5. Leaves informed, reassured, and empowered.

Benefits

For Passengers:

  • Accessible rights information
  • Simplified process
  • Reduced stress during disruptions

For Airlines:

  • Improved communication
  • Increased passenger trust
  • Reduced confusion and complaints

Limitations

  • Delays in real-time flight data may affect eligibility results.
  • Internet connectivity issues could hinder access.
  • Reliance on passengers entering accurate data.
  • Some cases still require clarification from airlines.

Scalability

  • Adaptation for regional markets
  • Partnerships with airlines and travel agencies
  • Integration across multiple platforms

Success Metrics

  • Website traffic, duration, and completed eligibility checks
  • User feedback on clarity and usefulness
  • Distribution and visibility of awareness materials
  • Increase in correctly filed compensation claims

Conclusion

The research highlighted that the real barrier in disrupted air travel is not compensation itself but the lack of awareness and clarity. By improving communication, simplifying documents, and making rights accessible, passengers can feel empowered and informed even in stressful travel situations.

Project Inform-Me-Right demonstrates how user-centered research and design can close the gap between passenger expectations and airline communication, ultimately creating a more transparent and trustworthy travel experience.

Clear communication builds trust — and trust is the real currency of travel. ✈️
