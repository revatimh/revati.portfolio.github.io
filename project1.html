Connecting Flights

Understanding Passenger Rights and Flight Disruption Experiences

A UX research project uncovering the communication gap between airlines and passengers during flight disruptions.

Overview

This research focuses on the communication gap between airlines and passengers, especially during flight disruptions. It examines the reasons behind the unawareness of passengers about their rights and compensation knowledge, which often leads to frustration and dissatisfaction.


By analyzing their expectations and experiences, the study aims to improve airline communication, reduce confusion, and enhance overall customer trust. Additionally, it proposes solutions to minimize issues, ensuring a smoother and more informed travel experience.

Problem Statement

What are the factors contributing to the communication gap between passengers' experiences and their expectations during a flight disruption?

Research Scope

107 Survey Responses
12 Passenger Interviews
4 Expert Interviews
18-30 Age Range

Understanding Our Participants

We focused on young international travelers who frequently experience connecting flights and are most affected by communication gaps during disruptions.

Research Objectives

Information Barriers

Identify information barriers for passengers and explore ways to establish clarity during stressful travel situations.

Experience Analysis

Analyze how past experiences shape passenger expectations and influence their reactions to disruptions.

Policy Review

Examine airline policies and passenger rights to improve cooperation and understanding between parties.

Expectation Gap

Assess whether passengers' experiences differ from expectations during disruptions and identify pain points.

Mixed-Methods Approach

We combined quantitative surveys with qualitative interviews to get both broad insights and deep understanding of passenger experiences.

Findings and Key Insights

1. Communication Gaps

Passengers lose trust due to insufficient information during disruptions.

"Knowing nothing is more concerning than knowing the truth."

Opportunity: Rebuild trust through transparent communication.

2. Lack of Rights Awareness

Many passengers were unaware of APPR (Air Passenger Protection Rights).

"I would have expected compensation but I assume this was not contemplated in the law."

Opportunity: Create awareness about passenger rights.

3. Complex Documentation

Only 21.9% of respondents read the APPR; most found it difficult to understand.

"It's like reading a textbook. It would be better if important points were highlighted."

Opportunity: Simplify the APPR and highlight key points.

4. Confusion Over Eligibility

56.1% of passengers were entitled to compensation, but only 29.2% pursued it.

"So it's like what needs to happen for me to have the right to get compensation?"

Opportunity: Develop tools to clarify eligibility and processes.

Conclusion

The research highlighted that the real barrier in disrupted air travel is not compensation itself but the lack of awareness and clarity. By improving communication, simplifying documents, and making rights accessible, passengers can feel empowered and informed even in stressful travel situations.


Project Inform-Me-Right demonstrates how user-centered research and design can close the gap between passenger expectations and airline communication, ultimately creating a more transparent and trustworthy travel experience.
