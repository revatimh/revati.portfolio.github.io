MediConnect

Simplifying healthcare appointments with ease and accessibility

Overview

This project aims to design a more user-friendly online healthcare appointment scheduling system that meets patient needs while improving administrative efficiency. Older adults show strong interest in health technologies, yet they face barriers such as small device screens, high costs, and frequent reliance on family for technical help. Many prefer step-by-step instructions and simple troubleshooting, highlighting the importance of intuitive design and clear guidance.

Research shows a decline in access to primary care in Canada, with millions lacking regular providers and same-day appointments remaining far below peer countries. Although many clinics offer online booking, adoption is still limited, with only a small portion of patients using these services. At the same time, feedback on virtual care is highly positive, especially among patients with chronic conditions.

Despite the availability of some digital tools like email communication and medical record access, many older adults and less tech-savvy users remain dependent on outdated phone systems. Existing platforms such as Porton Health, TELUS Health, and Scarborough Health Network demonstrate progress but also reveal fragmented systems and accessibility gaps. This project therefore responds to the growing demand for a unified, accessible, and widely adopted digital healthcare scheduling solution.

Problem Space

Usage of digital healthcare platforms by senior Citizens and individuals with limited technical knowledge is still lacking. Many digital healthcare platforms lack user-friendly design, multi-language support or accessibility features suitable for all age groups.

Opportunity Mapping

  • User-friendly design - Develop simple and streamlined user interfaces that cater specifically to the needs of older adults and individuals with limited tech skills.
  • Multi-language support - Implementing multi-language support to make platforms available to diverse communities.
  • Accessibility features - Features like screen readers, voice commands, and adjustable fonts will improve accessibility
  • Appointment - Alerts can be incorporated to inform about appointments beforehand.
  • Educational resources - Simple tutorials could be incorporated into the platforms to encourage usage

Value Proposition

Our platform offers a user-friendly digital healthcare solution with multi-language support, accessibility features, and automated appointment reminders. By prioritizing inclusive design and real-time scheduling, it ensures easier access, higher usability, and better adoption for seniors and individuals with limited tech skills.

Research Methodology

The project used a mixed-methods approach combining qualitative and quantitative research:

  • Literature review
  • Competitive analysis
  • Heuristic evaluation

Key Learnings

Usability Issues

Many platforms lack real-time updates, clear feedback, and intuitive navigation, leading to confusion during the booking process.

User Control & Freedom

Users struggle with modifying, canceling, or rescheduling appointments due to limited control over their actions.

Accessibility Gaps

Missing features like screen reader compatibility, language support, and voice commands reduce inclusivity.

Design Consistency

Inconsistent UI elements, overwhelming layouts, and cluttered screens impact user experience and decision-making.

Design Direction From Key Learnings

  • Simplified Booking Flow: Reduce steps, ensure real-time slot availability, and provide immediate confirmation to enhance usability.
  • Improved Navigation & Flexibility: Introduce back buttons, quick rebooking, and modification options for better user control.
  • Stronger Accessibility Features: Implement screen reader support, voice commands, and multi-language options for a more inclusive experience.
  • Minimalist & Consistent UI: Declutter screens, unify fonts/colors, and balance content to improve readability and engagement.

Initial Wireframes: Conceptualization

User Flow

Proposed Solution: Project Inform-Me-Right

Goals:

  1. Create awareness about APPR.
  2. Provide a website with:
    • Compensation eligibility checker
    • Simplified APPR guide

Awareness Campaigns

  • Posters and digital ads at airports
  • Brochures in seat pockets
  • In-flight entertainment system ads

Website Features

  • Eligibility Checker: Passengers input disruption details → get instant results.
  • Simplified APPR: Clear navigation, plain language, short sections, highlighted key points.

Storyboard (Solution in Action)

  1. Passenger experiences a delay and feels stressed.
  2. Notices promotional material for Inform-Me-Right.
  3. Scans QR code → accesses website.
  4. Reads simplified rights and checks eligibility.
  5. Leaves informed, reassured, and empowered.

Benefits

For Passengers:

  • Accessible rights information
  • Simplified process
  • Reduced stress during disruptions

For Airlines:

  • Improved communication
  • Increased passenger trust
  • Reduced confusion and complaints

Limitations

  • Delays in real-time flight data may affect eligibility results.
  • Internet connectivity issues could hinder access.
  • Reliance on passengers entering accurate data.
  • Some cases still require clarification from airlines.

Scalability

  • Adaptation for regional markets
  • Partnerships with airlines and travel agencies
  • Integration across multiple platforms

Success Metrics

  • Website traffic, duration, and completed eligibility checks
  • User feedback on clarity and usefulness
  • Distribution and visibility of awareness materials
  • Increase in correctly filed compensation claims

Conclusion

The research highlighted that the real barrier in disrupted air travel is not compensation itself but the lack of awareness and clarity. By improving communication, simplifying documents, and making rights accessible, passengers can feel empowered and informed even in stressful travel situations.

Project Inform-Me-Right demonstrates how user-centered research and design can close the gap between passenger expectations and airline communication, ultimately creating a more transparent and trustworthy travel experience.

Clear communication builds trust — and trust is the real currency of travel. ✈️
